Your data stays on your PC

AeroVRC is a local desktop app, not a service. Here’s exactly what it reads, stores and sends.

The short version: everything stays on your machine. No account is required, there’s no telemetry or tracking, and AeroVRC never uploads your data anywhere.

No account, no telemetry

The watchdog, dashboard, launcher, stats, performance tweaks and cache tools all work with no sign-in. AeroVRC doesn’t phone home, doesn’t include analytics, and doesn’t track you.

What it reads (all local, read-only)

  • VRChat’s own log files — to show your current world, instance and the “Who’s here” roster. This is information VRChat already writes to your disk.
  • Your VRCX database — opened read-only from a private snapshot copy so your friends and history can appear inside AeroVRC. Nothing is ever written back to VRCX.
  • Your VRChat photos folder — only to display thumbnails in the built-in gallery.
  • Basic system metrics — CPU, RAM, GPU and disk readings shown on the dashboard, read from your PC locally.

Where it stores things

Settings, logs, crash-log archives and photo thumbnails live in your Windows %APPDATA% folder. It’s a portable app — to remove it completely, delete AeroVRC.exe and (optionally) that folder.

The only times it touches the internet

  • GitHub — to check for and download updates and show the changelog. That’s a normal release check; no personal data is sent.
  • VRChat’s official API — only if you choose to log in on the optional Account page. Your credentials go directly to VRChat (api.vrchat.cloud) and nowhere else; 2FA is supported. The resulting session is stored on your PC encrypted with Windows DPAPI and is never sent to any third party. If you never log in, AeroVRC makes no VRChat API calls at all.
